Understanding Agency

From Action to Autonomy

What does it mean for a system—biological or artificial—to act with purpose?

The Spectrum of Agency

Agency exists on a continuum, from simple reactions to sophisticated autonomy.

1

Reactive

Responds to stimuli without planning

A thermostat turning on when temperature drops
2

Goal-Directed

Acts toward predefined objectives

A chess program maximizing win probability
3

Adaptive

Learns and adjusts strategies over time

An AI assistant improving from feedback
4

Autonomous

Sets own goals and determines actions

A human choosing their life direction

Components of Agency

True agency requires the integration of multiple capabilities.

🎯

Intentionality

The capacity to represent goals and pursue them

🧠

World Model

Internal representation of how the environment works

⚡

Action Space

Set of possible interventions in the world

🔄

Feedback Loop

Ability to perceive outcomes and adjust behavior

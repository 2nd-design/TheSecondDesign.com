## The Decision

Rahul noticed that juniors at his college had no exposure to design thinking, something he had picked up through online courses. Instead of complaining about the curriculum, he decided to organise a workshop himself. He had never run an event before. He had no budget, no team, and no permission from the department.

## The Steps

- **Step 1**: Wrote a one-page proposal and walked into his HOD's office. Got a tentative yes and a free classroom for a Saturday.
- **Step 2**: Created a Google Form and shared it in WhatsApp groups. 70 people signed up in two days.
- **Step 3**: Designed a 3-hour hands-on session. No slides longer than 5 minutes. Every activity had attendees making something.
- **Step 4**: Ran the workshop. 50 people showed up. The feedback form had an average rating of 4.6/5.

## What Happened

The department asked Rahul to run it again the next month. He recruited two juniors to help. By the end of the semester, they had run four workshops and created a small design thinking community at the college.

Rahul did not have experience, a title, or resources. He had a decision and a first step.
